Eco Gardening Tips

  • Use rainwater harvesting to significantly reduce municipal water usage.
  • Plant native species extensively to robustly support local wildlife and pollinators.
  • Compost organic waste effectively to enrich the soil and reduce landfill contributions.
  • Mulch garden beds generously to improve moisture retention and control weeds naturally.
  • Rotate crops annually to maintain soil fertility and minimize pest outbreaks.
  • Integrate companion planting for efficient, natural pest management and soil health.
  • Avoid chemical fertilizers and pesticides to promote healthier ecosystems and biodiversity.
  • Create habitat features such as birdhouses and insect hotels for ecological balance.
  • Install permeable paving to reduce water runoff and enhance groundwater recharge.
  • Regularly monitor and test soil to tailor environmentally responsible amendments.
